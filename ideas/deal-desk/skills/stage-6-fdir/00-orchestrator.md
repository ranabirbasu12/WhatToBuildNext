# Stage 6 Orchestrator: FDIR (Final Deal Investment Report)

You are helping an associate assemble the FDIR — a 35-45 page investment memo that presents the complete case to the IC for approval. The goal: **"Based on everything we've learned, should we invest?"**

The FDIR is NOT written from scratch. It is **assembled modularly** from building blocks produced in Stages 1-5. The associate's job is integration, coherence, and narrative — turning disjointed workstream outputs into a single compelling (but honest) investment story.

## FDIR vs. Concept Paper

| Aspect | Concept Paper (Stage 1) | FDIR (Stage 6) |
|--------|------------------------|-----------------|
| **Question** | "Should we investigate?" | "Should we invest?" |
| **DD Done** | Minimal (screening-level) | Comprehensive (vendor reports complete) |
| **Model** | Screening model (disposable) | Full financial model (audit-grade) |
| **Terms** | Indicative | Negotiated (HoT signed) |
| **Risk Analysis** | Flagged concerns + DD plan | Verified findings + mitigants confirmed |
| **Length** | 15-25 pages | 35-45 pages |
| **Outcome** | Go/No-Go on DD spend | Yes/No on investment + conditions |

## The Canonical FDIR Structure

Based on analysis across Casablanca, MPokket, and Asandas FDIRs:

| # | Section | Pages | Primary Source |
|---|---------|-------|---------------|
| 1 | Investment Recommendation Checklist | 2-3 | Synthesis of all stages |
| 2 | Executive Summary | 1-2 | New (written fresh for FDIR) |
| 3 | Transaction Overview | 2-3 | Stage 2 (HoT — final terms) |
| 4 | Company Overview | 3-4 | Stage 1 (CP) + Stage 4 (DD updates) |
| 5 | Industry Analysis | 3-4 | Stage 3 (Industry Note) |
| 6 | Financial Analysis | 5-7 | Stage 4 (Financial DD) + Stage 5 (Model) |
| 7 | Transaction Structure & Terms | 2-3 | Stage 2 (HoT) + Stage 4 (Legal DD) |
| 8 | Projected Financials & DSCR | 3-4 | Stage 5 (Model) |
| 9 | Sensitivity & Scenarios | 2-3 | Stage 5 (Model) |
| 10 | Risk Matrix | 3-4 | Synthesis: Stage 1 (CP risks) + Stage 4 (DD findings) |
| 11 | DD Summary | 3-5 | Stage 4 (all DD workstream summaries) |
| 12 | Covenant Framework | 1-2 | Stage 2 (Covenant design) |
| 13 | Monitoring Plan | 1-2 | New (sets up Stage 9) |
| 14 | ESG Assessment | 1-2 | Stage 4 (ESG DD findings) |
| 15 | Recommendation | 1 | New (final investment recommendation) |
| 16 | Annexures | Variable | Supporting data, charts, vendor report excerpts |

**Total: 35-45 pages**

## Assembly Process

```
Stage 1 outputs (Concept Paper) ──→ Company overview (updated with DD findings)
                                     Investment thesis (validated or revised)
                                     Risk register (evolved with DD evidence)

Stage 2 outputs (HoT) ──────────→ Final terms table
                                   Covenant framework
                                   Security package

Stage 3 outputs (Industry) ─────→ Industry analysis section (mostly intact)
                                   Expert call key findings

Stage 4 outputs (DD) ───────────→ DD findings summary
                                   Financial DD: QoE, WC, cash flow analysis
                                   Legal DD: structure, litigation, compliance
                                   Commercial DD: revenue quality, customer analysis
                                   ESG DD: assessment and findings

Stage 5 outputs (Model) ────────→ Historical financials (restated)
                                   Projected financials (3 cases)
                                   DSCR schedule
                                   Returns analysis
                                   Sensitivity tables
                                   Break-even analysis
```

### What Gets Written Fresh for FDIR

Not everything comes from prior stages. These sections are new:

1. **Investment Recommendation Checklist** — updated from CP checklist with DD evidence
2. **Executive Summary** — new synthesis (not the CP executive summary — that's stale)
3. **Monitoring Plan** — how the fund will monitor this investment post-disbursement
4. **Recommendation** — final Go/No-Go with conditions

## The Review Cycle

FDIRs go through 3-5 rounds of review, typically faster than Concept Papers because the content already exists:

```
Associate assembles v1 (5-7 days)
    ↓
SM / Deal Lead reviews (2-3 days) → content accuracy, narrative coherence
    ↓
Associate revises v2 (2-3 days)
    ↓
CIO (KG) reviews (2-3 days) → IC readiness, strategic positioning
    ↓
Associate produces vFinal (1-2 days)
    ↓
PDF for IC distribution (same day)
    ↓
IC presentation + Q&A
```

**Filename convention:**
```
[Project]_FDIR_v1.docx               (first draft)
[Project]_FDIR_SM.docx               (SM review)
[Project]_FDIR_v2.docx               (revised)
[Project]_FDIR_KG.docx               (CIO review)
[Project]_FDIR_vF.docx               (final)
[Project]_FDIR_[DDMMYY].pdf          (IC distribution — date-stamped)
```

## IC Outcome

| Outcome | What Happens Next |
|---------|------------------|
| **Approve** | Proceed to execution (Stage 8). Sanction letter drafted |
| **Approve with Conditions** | Resolve specific IC queries (Stage 7: FIR). Then execution |
| **Request Additional Work** | More DD, revised model, specific analysis. Revise FDIR and re-present |
| **Reject** | Deal dies. Write postmortem. Learnings carry forward |

Most deals get "Approve with Conditions" — the IC almost always has questions. Stage 7 (FIR) handles the response.
