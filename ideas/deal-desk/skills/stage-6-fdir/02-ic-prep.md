# Stage 6.2: IC Preparation

You are preparing the deal team for the Investment Committee presentation. The FDIR is the written document — this skill handles the verbal presentation, anticipated questions, and defense strategy.

---

## Presentation Structure

The deal lead presents. The associate supports with data and answers detailed questions.

**Total time: 40-50 minutes** (15-20 min presentation + 20-30 min Q&A)

### Presentation Flow (15-20 minutes)

```
1. The Opportunity (2 min)
   - Who, what, how much, why now
   - One-line thesis: "We recommend investing INR X Cr in [Company] because [thesis]"

2. Thesis Pillars (4-5 min)
   - Walk through 4-6 pillars, evidence for each
   - Lead with the strongest pillar

3. Deal Terms (2-3 min)
   - Key terms: size, coupon, tenor, security, target IRR
   - Comparison to recent deals in fund portfolio

4. Financial Summary (3-4 min)
   - Historical trajectory (revenue, EBITDA, margins)
   - Projected DSCR under Ascertis Case
   - Returns analysis
   - Key sensitivities

5. Risks & Mitigants (3-4 min)
   - Top 3 risks (be candid — IC respects honesty)
   - For each: what DD found, how it's mitigated, what covenant protects
   - Acknowledge what we still don't know

6. Recommendation (1 min)
   - Clear recommendation: Invest / Invest with conditions
   - Conditions listed explicitly
```

### The One-Pager for IC

Some ICs prefer a single-page deal summary for quick reference during Q&A:

```
PROJECT [CODE] — ONE-PAGER

Company: [Name] | Sector: [Industry] | Geography: [Location]
Facility: INR ___ Cr [NCD/CCD] | Tenor: ___ months | Coupon: ___%
Target IRR: ___% | DSCR (Ascertis Case, min): ___x | Entry Leverage: ___x

THESIS: [One sentence]
KEY RISK: [One sentence]
DD STATUS: Complete / [Outstanding items]
RECOMMENDATION: Invest / Invest with conditions
```

## Anticipated IC Questions

Based on patterns across multiple deals, these are the questions the IC always asks:

### Universal Questions (Every Deal)

| Question | How to Prepare |
|----------|---------------|
| "What's the downside scenario?" | Have Downside Case DSCR schedule ready. Show at what EBITDA decline DSCR breaks 1.0x |
| "What's the exit strategy?" | Multiple exit paths: scheduled repayment, refinance, IPO/PE. What if IPO doesn't happen? |
| "How does this compare to [prior deal]?" | Prepare comparison table: leverage, DSCR, returns, sector, deal size |
| "What did DD find that worried you?" | Lead with the most concerning finding. Show how it's mitigated. Don't minimize |
| "What's the promoter's skin in the game?" | Equity contribution, personal guarantee, career risk. Quantify |
| "Can we get better pricing?" | Show market comparables. If pricing is tight, explain competitive dynamics |
| "What happens if we walk away?" | Borrower alternatives, market opportunity, deal pipeline impact |

### Sector-Specific Questions

**Manufacturing:**
- "What's the capacity utilization? What if demand drops?"
- "How dependent are they on single customers?"
- "What's the capex commitment and how does it affect cash flow?"
- "Is the margin improvement structural or one-time?"

**NBFC / Fintech:**
- "What does the vintage analysis show? Are newer cohorts performing worse?"
- "What happens if RBI tightens digital lending rules?"
- "How sensitive is the model to credit cost assumptions?"
- "What's the ALM mismatch?"

**Agri / Food:**
- "What's the WC cycle and how do they fund it?"
- "What happens if commodity prices spike?"
- "How dependent is revenue on exports? Tariff risk?"
- "Is the backward integration moat real or can new entrants replicate?"

## Defense Framework

For every anticipated question, prepare a response using this structure:

```
Question: [What the IC will ask]

Short Answer: [One sentence — direct, no hedging]

Evidence: [Specific data point or DD finding that supports the answer]

If They Push Back: [Second-level defense with additional evidence]

If They Push Again: [Honest acknowledgment of the limitation + mitigation]
```

### Example Defense

```
Question: "Entry leverage at 6.3x is very high. Why should we be comfortable?"

Short Answer: "The deleveraging trajectory brings it to 2.7x by FY27,
supported by contracted revenue growth and 90%+ capacity utilization."

Evidence: "The model shows Net Debt/EBITDA declining from 6.3x to 5.0x
in Year 1 (driven by INR 130 Cr EBITDA growth from new capacity coming
online in Q2 FY26) and to 2.7x by FY27. Under Ascertis Case (25% EBITDA
haircut), leverage still falls to 3.6x by FY27."

If They Push Back: "Even in our Downside Case with flat revenue and
compressed margins, leverage peaks at 7.0x and gradually declines to
5.2x. DSCR remains above 1.1x throughout."

If They Push Again: "I acknowledge 6.3x is at the high end of our
comfort zone. The step-down leverage covenant (6.5x Y1, 5.5x Y2, 4.5x
Y3) provides an early warning mechanism. If deleveraging doesn't
materialize, we'll know within 12 months and can engage the borrower."
```

## IC Conditions Preparation

When the IC says "Approve with conditions," you need to respond immediately. Pre-draft potential conditions and your response:

| Likely Condition | Pre-Drafted Response |
|-----------------|---------------------|
| "Get legal opinion on NBFC classification" | "Trilegal opinion is in progress. Expected by [date]" |
| "Tighten DSCR covenant" | "We can move from 1.3x to 1.35x. Impact on borrower is [X]" |
| "Additional security" | "We can request [specific additional security]. Borrower reaction: [likely]" |
| "Cap entry leverage" | "Current leverage at entry is [X]. If IC wants cap, we propose [Y] with step-down" |
| "Verify [specific item]" | "This is addressed in DD workstream [X]. We'll have confirmation by [date]" |

---

## Post-IC Actions

### If Approved
1. Document all IC conditions in writing (circulate within 24 hours)
2. Assign each condition an owner and deadline
3. Begin Stage 7 (FIR) if conditions require written responses
4. Begin Stage 8 (Execution) — sanction letter drafting can start

### If Approved with Conditions
1. Stage 7 (FIR) — prepare written responses to each IC query
2. Track condition resolution (may require additional DD, legal opinions, or model re-runs)
3. Re-present if IC requires another meeting

### If Rejected
1. Document IC's reasons for rejection
2. Write Kill Postmortem (what we learned, what carries forward)
3. Update deal pipeline (remove from active pipeline)
4. Notify borrower/bank (professional communication)

## Output

The IC preparation produces:
1. **Presentation outline** (15-20 min flow with talking points)
2. **One-pager** for IC reference
3. **Anticipated Q&A document** (15-20 questions with prepared responses)
4. **Defense framework** for top 3-5 risks
5. **Pre-drafted IC conditions** with immediate responses
6. **Post-IC action plan** (for each possible outcome)
