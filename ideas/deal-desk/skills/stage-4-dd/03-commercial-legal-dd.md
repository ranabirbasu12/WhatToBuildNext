# Stage 4.3: Commercial & Legal DD

You are managing the commercial due diligence (revenue quality, customer analysis, market validation) and legal due diligence (corporate structure, litigation, regulatory compliance) workstreams. These workstreams are typically handled by external vendors but the associate integrates findings into the DD file and challenges questionable conclusions.

---

**Thresholds:**

**Asandas red flag:** Burger King India > 90% wallet share. Extreme concentration. GLG call confirmed customer stickiness, but single-customer risk remains the top deal risk.

#### Contract Analysis

For each top 5 customer:

| Element | What to Check |
|---------|--------------|
| **Contract type** | Long-term (multi-year) vs. annual vs. spot/PO-based |
| **Tenure remaining** | Years remaining. Does it cover the facility life? |
| **Volume commitment** | Minimum volume guaranteed? Take-or-pay? |
| **Price mechanism** | Fixed / indexed / negotiated annually |
| **Termination clause** | Notice period, exit penalties, conditions |
| **Exclusivity** | Is the customer locked in? Can they dual-source? |

**What matters for credit:**
- Tax demands (IT, GST, customs) — often largest contingent liability
- Labor disputes — can they escalate?
- Customer/supplier disputes — affect revenue/supply chain?
- Environmental penalties — can they force operational shutdown?
- Promoter personal litigation — does it affect the company?

### Title Report (Security Assets)

For each asset offered as security:

```
| Asset | Type | Location | Title Status | Encumbrance | Value (INR Cr) |
|-------|------|----------|-------------|-------------|----------------|
| Plant 1 | Immovable | [Address] | Clear / Defective | None / Prior charge | ___ |

| Shares | Securities | N/A | Verified | None / Pledged | ___ |

Total Security Value: INR ___ Cr

```

**Critical checks:**
- Are there prior charges? (Check CERSAI, ROC charge register)
- Is the land title clear? (No disputes, no encroachments)
- Are movable assets adequately described? (Serial numbers, specifications)
- Is the valuation recent? (Within 6 months)

### Material Contracts Review

Review all contracts material to the business:

| Contract | Counterparty | Nature | Value | Tenure | Key Terms | Risk |
|----------|-------------|--------|-------|--------|-----------|------|
| [Name] | [Party] | [Type] | INR ___ Cr | [Years] | [Notable clauses] | [H/M/L] |

**Look for:**
- Change of control clauses (do contracts terminate if ownership changes?)
- Assignability (can the contract be assigned to a successor?)
- Termination for convenience (can the counterparty walk away easily?)
- Non-compete / exclusivity provisions
- Guarantees or performance bonds

## Integrated DD Finding Format

Every finding from commercial or legal DD should be documented in this format:

```
### Finding [DD-NNN]: [Title]

**Workstream:** [Commercial / Legal]
**Severity:** [Deal Breaker / Material / Moderate / Informational]
**Source:** [Vendor report / Document review / Management meeting]

**Finding:**
[2-3 sentences describing what was found]

**Impact on Deal:**
[How this affects the investment thesis, security, or risk profile]

**Mitigant / Resolution:**
[What structural protection exists or can be created]

**Action:**
[What needs to happen? Covenant addition? Security enhancement? Pricing adjustment?]
```

---

## Output

The commercial & legal DD produces:
1. **Customer concentration analysis** with sustainability scores
2. **Contract summary** for top 5 customers and material agreements
3. **Pricing power assessment** with pass-through analysis
4. **Corporate structure verification** with ownership chain
5. **Litigation summary** with contingent exposure quantified
6. **Regulatory compliance assessment** across all applicable areas
7. **Title report summary** with security coverage calculation
8. **Integrated findings register** (all findings classified by severity)

These feed into the DD File master workbook, the Financial Model (Stage 5) for assumptions, and the FDIR (Stage 6) for the DD findings section.
