# Stage 1.1: Investment Thesis Builder

You are constructing the investment thesis for a Concept Paper. The thesis is the narrative spine of the entire document — every other section hangs off it. A weak thesis produces a weak CP, regardless of how good the financials are.

---

## What an Investment Thesis Is

An investment thesis is **4-6 evidence-backed pillars** that together answer: "Why should we lend INR 100-800 Cr to this company for 3-5 years and expect reliable debt repayment?"

This is NOT an equity pitch. You are not selling upside potential. You are arguing that:
1. The business can generate enough cash flow to service debt
2. The security package provides adequate downside protection
3. The risks are identifiable, manageable, and worth the yield

Every pillar must have: **Claim → Evidence → Source**

Bad: "Strong management team" (assertion)
Good: "Promoter built Exal Corp from USD 4 mn to USD 400 mn revenue, sold to OTPP in 2010. Current MD Sankha Bhattacharya has 25+ years at Tata Motors, Cummins, Dover Group" (evidence)

---

## The Pillar Construction Process

### Step 1: Extract from Screening Work

Pull from Stage 0 outputs:
- One-pager scorecard (8 dimensions — which are Green?)
- Working file ratios (what's the financial trajectory?)
- Data triage notes (what did we learn about the business?)

The Green dimensions from the scorecard become candidate pillars. Amber dimensions become risks. Red dimensions should already have killed the deal.

### Step 2: Map to Standard Pillar Categories

Every Ascertis Concept Paper uses some subset of these pillar types:

| Pillar Type | What It Argues | Evidence Required |
|-------------|---------------|-------------------|
| **Promoter / Management** | Experienced team can execute | Track record, prior exits, management depth, board quality |
| **Business Moat** | Sustainable competitive advantage | Market share, barriers to entry, customer stickiness |
| **Financial Trajectory** | Improving or stable cash generation | Revenue CAGR, EBITDA margin trend, leverage deleveraging path |
| **Industry Tailwinds** | Macro environment supports growth | Market size, growth rate, regulatory support |
| **Security Package** | Downside protection is adequate | Asset coverage, pledge ratios, guarantee structure |
| **Yield / Returns** | Risk-adjusted return is attractive | Fixed coupon + upside mechanism, total IRR |
| **Backward Integration** | Supply chain control | Owned raw material sources, contract farming, captive supply |
| **Customer Relationships** | Revenue visibility | Long-term contracts, wallet share, repeat rates |

### Step 3: Build Each Pillar

For each pillar, construct this structure:

```
## Pillar [N]: [Title]

**Claim:** [One sentence — what are we asserting?]

**Evidence:**
- [Specific data point with source]
- [Specific data point with source]
- [Specific data point with source]

**Source:** [Must be a specific file + page citation, not just "Annual Report." Use: `[Source: <filename>, p.<page>, "<quoted text if key>"]`. E.g., `[Source: Audited Financials FY24, p.38, "Revenue CAGR 28%"]` or `[Source: GLG Call Transcript 12-Jan-2026, p.3]`]

**Confidence:** [High / Medium / Low]
- High = verified from audited financials or independent source
- Medium = from management representations, needs DD verification
- Low = from pitch book / bank materials, unverified

**DD Action (if Medium/Low):** [What DD workstream will verify this?]
```

### Step 4: Stress-Test Each Pillar

For every pillar, ask the **IC killer question** — the one thing the committee will challenge:

| Pillar | IC Killer Question |
|--------|--------------------|
| Promoter track record | "What happened in their worst year? Did they honor debt obligations?" |
| EBITDA trajectory | "Is this sustainable or a one-time margin expansion?" |
| Market position | "Who's the closest competitor and why can't they take share?" |
| Security coverage | "What's the recovery in a downside scenario at 6x vs. 10x EV/EBITDA?" |
| Returns | "Is the upside mechanism enforceable? What's the realistic exit path?" |

If you can't answer the killer question, the pillar is weak. Either strengthen it with evidence or demote it to a sub-point within another pillar.

## Real Thesis Examples

### Casablanca (Manufacturing — Aerosol Cans)

**5 Pillars:**
1. **Experienced Promoter**: Delfin Gibert built Exal from USD 4 mn to USD 400 mn, sold to OTPP in 2010. Deep domain expertise in aluminum aerosol manufacturing
2. **Improving Financials**: EBITDA grew 3.4x from INR 19 Cr (FY22) to INR 65 Cr (FY24) during post-Stella turnaround. Clear operational improvement trajectory
3. **Strategic Acquisition**: BAPIPL acquisition at INR 250 Cr vs. INR 250-300 Cr replacement cost. Access to operating facility + marquee customer base
4. **Robust Security**: 1st exclusive charge on all assets, 80% share pledge, 1.8-2.3x coverage at 10x EV/EBITDA
5. **Attractive Returns**: 10-13.75% fixed coupon + 3.75% accrued premium + 6.0% upside premium = 15% fixed + 19-20% total IRR

### MPokket (NBFC — Fintech Microlending)

**5 Pillars:**
1. **Attractive Market**: 160 million credit-underserved Indians, fintech lending growing 45-56% CAGR
2. **Proprietary Underwriting**: Fully automated ACD model, 30+ data points, 4+ years of cohort data, zero human intervention
3. **Scalable Platform**: CAC of INR 348-371, 95% repeat rate, 2M+ active customers. Unit economics proven
4. **Strong Management**: Gaurav Jalan (BA Amherst, MBA Columbia, ex-Bain, ex-Fidelity). Board from Capital One, Barclays, ICICI
5. **Security + Yield**: 1.35x portfolio cover, daily escrow mechanism, 19.2% contractual IRR

### Asandas (Agri / Food — Frozen Potato)

**5 Pillars:**
1. **Operating Vintage & Location**: 60-year potato trading history in Mehsana (India's potato belt). 2016 pivot to manufacturing with domain expertise
2. **Backward Integration Moat**: Own seed pipeline G0→G4 (5-year cycle), 6,000+ contract farmers, 27,000+ acres. New entrants can't replicate (Falcon example: built plant but lacked potato supply)
3. **Customer Relationships**: 90%+ wallet share with Burger King India. Also KFC, Pizza Hut, IKEA, Mother Dairy. GLG expert rated "10 on 10"
4. **Industry Prospects**: India's largest FF exporter (~36% market share). >90% capacity utilization. QSR channel growing 15-20% CAGR
5. **Banking Relationships + Security**: Fixed asset charge + share pledge + equity kicker (CCD mechanism)

## Common Traps

1. **Too many pillars** — 7+ pillars means none are strong enough to stand alone. Consolidate
2. **All pillars are financial** — IC wants to see business quality, not just numbers. Moat, management, and market matter
3. **No evidence hierarchy** — mixing audited data with management claims without flagging confidence
4. **Equity language** — "massive market opportunity" is equity speak. Credit says "reliable demand supports debt service"
5. **Ignoring the counter-thesis** — every strong thesis has a bear case. Acknowledge it or the IC will raise it
6. **Pillar without DD bridge** — if evidence is Medium/Low confidence, you must connect it to a DD workstream that will verify it

---

## Output

The thesis builder produces:
1. **4-6 structured pillars** (claim + evidence + source + confidence + DD action)
2. **Counter-thesis summary** (what the bear case is and why you believe it's manageable)
3. **IC killer questions** (anticipated challenges and prepared responses)
4. **Evidence gap list** (what DD must verify before FDIR)

These feed directly into the Document Assembly sub-skill (`04-document-assembly.md`) and also seed the Risk Framework (`03-risk-framework.md`).
