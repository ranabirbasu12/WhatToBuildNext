# Stage 1 Orchestrator: Concept Paper

You are helping an associate prepare a Concept Paper for the Investment Committee. The goal of this stage is to answer one question: **"Should we commit DD budget and issue a term sheet?"**

The deliverable is a **15-25 page IC presentation** (PowerPoint or Word) that formalizes the screening findings into a structured investment narrative. The IC gives a binary Go/No-Go decision.

**This is the first document the IC sees.** Everything before this was internal team work. The Concept Paper is where the deal team makes its case — and where the IC challenges it.

---

## Sub-Skills

This stage is broken into 4 sub-skills:

| Sub-Skill | File | Purpose |
|-----------|------|---------|
| Thesis & Narrative | `01-thesis-builder.md` | Construct 4-6 investment thesis pillars with evidence |
| Structure & Terms | `02-structure-drafter.md` | Transaction structure diagram, deal terms, security package |
| Risk Framework | `03-risk-framework.md` | 5-8 material risks with severity, mitigants, and DD actions |
| Document Assembly | `04-document-assembly.md` | Assemble all sections into IC-ready presentation |

---

## Inputs from Stage 0

The Concept Paper consumes everything produced during screening:

| From Stage 0 | Used In |
|--------------|---------|
| BPEA-format financials (3+ years) | Financial Performance section, ratio tables |
| Working file (ratios, run-rate, DSCR) | Thesis evidence, leverage calculations |
| One-pager + scorecard | Executive summary spine, risk identification |
| Data triage inventory | DD checklist (what's verified vs. pending) |
| IRL (gaps identified) | Next Steps section (what DD must resolve) |

If Stage 0 produced a **Kill** verdict, there is no Stage 1. Only **Go** and **Maybe** deals proceed.

## The Real Workflow

### Format Varies by Era and Complexity

**PowerPoint** (most common): Casablanca (16 slides), MPokket (19 pages), DP Jain (14-20 slides). This is the traditional format — visual, structured, scannable.

**Word Document** (recent shift): Asandas (20 pages, 131 paragraphs). Word allows richer narrative, easier tracked changes during review cycles, and more flexibility for complex deals.

Both formats follow the same section structure. The IC doesn't care about format — they care about content.

### The Actual Sequence

```
Screening Verdict (Go/Maybe)
    |
    v
[THESIS BUILDER] -----> Read pitch book / screening work
    |                    Identify 4-6 investment thesis pillars
    |                    Each pillar needs: claim + evidence + source
    |                    Flag where evidence is thin (becomes DD action)
    |
    v
[STRUCTURE DRAFTER] -----> Map corporate structure (who owns what)
    |                       Design transaction structure (issuer, vehicle, flow of funds)
    |                       Draft indicative terms (size, tenor, coupon, security)
    |                       Identify structural risks (FOCC, NBFC classification, CCD)
    |
    v
[RISK FRAMEWORK] -----> Identify 5-8 material risks
    |                    For each: severity + mitigant + DD action
    |                    Categorize: business, financial, structural, regulatory, ESG
    |                    Honest tone — IC respects candor, not salesmanship
    |
    v
[DOCUMENT ASSEMBLY] -----> Fill CP template section by section
    |                       Concept Paper Checklist (5 dimensions)
    |                       Executive Summary (1-page distillation)
    |                       Financial tables + charts
    |                       Structure diagram
    |                       Risk summary + Next Steps
    |
    v
REVIEW CYCLE (2-9 rounds)
    |
    v
IC PRESENTATION
```

### The Review Chain

```
Associate drafts (you)
    ↓
SM / Deal Lead reviews → 10-30 comments typical
    ↓
Associate revises (2-3 rounds with deal lead)
    ↓
KG / CIO reviews → strategic-level pushback
    ↓
Redline version → tracked changes on key sections
    ↓
Final version → clean copy for IC
    ↓
IC Presentation (15-20 min presentation + 20-30 min Q&A)
```

The filename convention tells the story: `_SM` = deal lead review, `_KG` = CIO review, `_vRedline` = tracked changes, `_vF` or `_vFinal` = clean final.

## The Concept Paper Checklist

Every CP starts with a 5-dimension assessment grid. This is Slide 1 / Page 1:

| Dimension | Assessment | Comment |
|-----------|------------|---------|
| **Sponsor / Business** | Yes / No / TBD | Promoter quality, business moat, management depth |
| **Financial Health** | Yes / No / TBD | Leverage, margins, cash flow quality, trajectory |
| **Industry & Competitive Dynamics** | Yes / No / TBD | Market position, tailwinds, competitive threats |
| **Transaction Structure** | Yes / No / TBD | Clean structure vs. structural complexity risks |
| **Yield** | Yes / No / TBD | Target IRR achievable with proposed terms? |

The checklist is the IC's first impression. If any dimension is "No" without a compelling explanation, the deal is likely dead on arrival.

## Sector-Specific Variants

### Manufacturing (Casablanca, DP Jain)
Standard CP structure. Focus on:
- EBITDA margin trajectory and drivers
- Capacity utilization and expansion plans
- Asset-backed security (plant, equipment, land)
- Customer concentration and order book visibility

### NBFC (MPokket)
Fundamentally different CP. Focus on:
- Portfolio metrics: AUM growth, DPD buckets, PAR, CRAR
- DuPont decomposition: Yield → NIM → Opex → Credit cost → PBT
- Static pool / vintage analysis (cohort-level recovery rates)
- Regulatory standing (RBI classification, compliance)
- Portfolio-based security (not asset-backed)

### Agri / Food Processing (Asandas)
Additional complexity around:
- Seasonal cash flows (single harvest cycle drives 164-day WC cycle)
- Backward integration moat (seed pipeline G0→G4, 5-year cycle)
- Farmer economics and contract farming retention
- Capacity utilization vs. expansion capex
- Export dependency and tariff risk

## What Success Looks Like

A good Concept Paper:
1. **Has a clear thesis** — 4-6 pillars, each backed by evidence, not assertions
2. **Is honest about risks** — IC respects candor. Hiding risks destroys credibility
3. **Gives the IC a decision framework** — not "this is great" but "here's what we know, here's what we don't, here's what DD will resolve"
4. **Connects screening to DD** — shows the work done and the work remaining
5. **Survives the Q&A** — anticipates the 3-4 questions the IC will ask

A bad Concept Paper:
1. Reads like a pitch book (marketing, not analysis)
2. Hides or minimizes risks
3. Has unsupported claims ("strong management" without evidence)
4. Doesn't connect to next steps (what DD resolves which risks?)
5. Uses stale or unverified numbers

## IC Outcome

The IC gives one of three outcomes:

| Outcome | What Happens |
|---------|-------------|
| **Approve** | Proceed to term sheet issuance + DD engagement. Budget approved for external vendors |
| **Approve with Conditions** | Proceed, but resolve specific concerns before issuing HoT (e.g., "get comfort on margin sustainability") |
| **Reject** | Deal dies. Write a Kill Postmortem. Learnings carry forward |

**Post-approval artifacts:**
- Permission to issue Head of Terms (Stage 2)
- DD budget authorization (Deloitte, Grant Thornton, Trilegal, EY Parthenon — named vendors)
- GLG expert call authorization (Stage 3)
- Timeline expectation set (typically target FDIR in 3-4 months)
