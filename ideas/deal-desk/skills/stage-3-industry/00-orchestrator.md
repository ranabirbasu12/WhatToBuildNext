# Stage 3 Orchestrator: Industry & Expert Calls

You are helping an associate produce an industry note and manage GLG/expert network calls for a deal. The goal of this stage is: **"Does the sector support reliable debt repayment over the facility life?"**

The deliverables are an **Industry Note (5-10 pages)** and **structured notes from 1-3 expert calls**.

**This stage runs in PARALLEL with Stage 2 (HoT negotiation).** Neither blocks the other. Industry findings may influence covenant design in the HoT, and HoT terms may inform which industry questions matter most.

---

## Why This Stage Exists

The Concept Paper (Stage 1) included a brief industry section — just enough for the IC to understand the sector. Stage 3 goes deep. The industry note and expert calls answer questions the IC raised and provide the analytical foundation for:

- **Stage 4 (DD)**: Commercial DD workstream (market position validation)
- **Stage 5 (Model)**: Revenue growth assumptions, margin benchmarks
- **Stage 6 (FDIR)**: Dedicated industry analysis section

---

## The Real Workflow

```
IC approves Concept Paper
    |
    v
[INDUSTRY NOTE] -----> Desk research: market reports, trade data, regulatory filings
    |                   Peer company analysis (public filings, credit ratings)
    |                   Sector-specific risk mapping
    |
    |  (in parallel)
    |
[EXPERT CALLS] -----> Draft GLG/expert network brief
    |                  Schedule 1-3 calls (industry practitioner, competitor, customer)
    |                  Conduct calls with structured question framework
    |                  Produce call notes → validate/challenge industry thesis
    |
    v
Industry Note finalized
    |
    v
Feeds into DD (Stage 4) and FDIR (Stage 6)
```

### Expert Call Types

| Call Type | Who You Talk To | What You Learn |
|-----------|----------------|----------------|
| **Industry Practitioner** | Senior executive in the sector (not the borrower) | Market dynamics, competitive landscape, growth outlook |
| **Competitor Perspective** | Executive at a competing company | Competitive threats, pricing dynamics, market share shifts |
| **Customer Perspective** | Key customer of the borrower | Stickiness, switching costs, satisfaction, alternatives |
| **Regulatory Expert** | Consultant or former regulator | Regulatory trajectory, compliance burden, policy risk |

**Example (Asandas):** GLG call with Burger King India supply chain executive → rated Hyfun "10 on 10" for quality, noted quick decision-making vs. McCain's rigidity. This became Pillar 3 evidence in the thesis.
