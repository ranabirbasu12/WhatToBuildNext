# Stage 3.2: Expert Call Manager

You are managing GLG (or other expert network) calls for the deal. Expert calls provide practitioner perspectives that desk research cannot — competitive intelligence, customer stickiness, regulatory trajectory, and operational realities.

---

## The Expert Call Lifecycle

```
Identify knowledge gaps (from CP risk register + IC questions)
    ↓
Draft expert brief (1 page — company context, what we need to learn)
    ↓
GLG/network matches experts (2-5 candidates)
    ↓
Review expert profiles → select 1-3 for calls
    ↓
Prepare question framework (structured, prioritized)
    ↓
Conduct call (45-60 min each)
    ↓
Produce structured call notes
    ↓
Synthesize findings → update industry thesis
```

---

## The Expert Brief

Send to GLG/expert network to find the right experts:

```
## Expert Network Brief

**Project Code:** [Codename]
**Sector:** [Industry]
**Geography:** India

### Company Context
[2-3 sentences about the borrower — what they do, market position, deal context]
[DO NOT disclose: fund name, deal terms, investment amount, internal analysis]

### Expert Profile Sought
[Type of expert needed — competitor, customer, industry analyst, regulator]

### Key Questions (Priority Order)

3. [Third priority]

### Compliance Notes
- No discussion of material non-public information
- Expert must not be a current employee of [borrower] or [its direct customers]

```

## Question Framework by Expert Type

### Industry Practitioner (Competitor or Adjacent Player)

**Market Dynamics:**
- What's the realistic growth rate for this segment over next 3-5 years?
- What's driving growth — is it structural or cyclical?
- What's the biggest threat to incumbents in this space?

**Competitive Landscape:**
- Who are the top 3-4 players? What's their relative positioning?
- Is there meaningful capacity coming online in the next 2-3 years?
- How sticky are customer relationships? What does it take to win an account away?

**Operational:**
- What are typical margins in this business? What drives margin variability?
- What's the capital intensity? How much capex is needed to maintain position?
- What are the key operational risks? (Supply chain, labor, technology)

**Credit-Specific:**
- In a downturn scenario, how much volume decline would you expect?
- Have you seen companies in this space face debt service pressure? What caused it?
- What's the typical working capital cycle? Any seasonal patterns?

### Customer Perspective

**Relationship:**
- How long have you worked with [borrower]?
- What would it take for you to switch to a competitor? (Cost, disruption, time)
- How would you rate their product/service quality on a scale of 1-10? Why?

**Wallet Share:**
- What percentage of your [product] needs does [borrower] supply?
- Are you diversifying your supplier base? Why or why not?
- What's your planned growth in this product category?

**Competitive Alternatives:**
- Who else could supply this product? At what quality and price?
- If [borrower] had capacity constraints, where would you go?
- What does [borrower] do better/worse than alternatives?

### Regulatory Expert (esp. for NBFC)

**Current Framework:**
- What are the key regulatory constraints in this sector?
- How is regulatory enforcement trending? (Tighter, stable, relaxing)
- What recent regulatory changes have had the biggest impact?

**Forward-Looking:**
- What regulatory changes do you expect in the next 2-3 years?
- Is there a risk of material policy change that could affect this business model?
- How would [specific scenario] affect companies in this space?

## Synthesizing Multiple Calls

If 2-3 calls are conducted, produce a synthesis:

```
## Expert Call Synthesis

**Calls Conducted:** [N]
**Experts:** [Industry practitioner, Customer, Regulatory — types]

### Consensus Views (All Experts Agree)
- [Finding that all experts validated]
- [Another consensus finding]

### Divergent Views (Experts Disagree)
- [Topic]: Expert A says [X], Expert B says [Y]
  - **Our Assessment:** [Which view is more credible and why]

### New Information (Not in Our Prior Analysis)
- [Something we didn't know before these calls]
- [Another new finding]

### Updated Industry Assessment
- **Before Calls:** [SUPPORTIVE / NEUTRAL / ADVERSE]
- **After Calls:** [SUPPORTIVE / NEUTRAL / ADVERSE]
- **Key Change:** [What shifted and why]
```

## Common Pitfalls

1. **Leading questions**: "Don't you think this company has a strong moat?" → Expert will agree to be polite. Ask: "What would it take for a new entrant to compete effectively?"
2. **Confirmation bias**: Don't just seek experts who will validate your thesis. Include at least one skeptical perspective
3. **Over-reliance on single expert**: One person's view is an anecdote. Two agreeing views are a data point. Three are a pattern
4. **Forgetting credit lens**: Expert calls in PE focus on growth. In credit, focus on downside: "What could cause this company to miss debt payments?"
5. **Poor note-taking**: Real-time notes during the call. Structured notes within 24 hours. Memory degrades fast

## Output

The expert call manager produces:
1. **Expert brief** (for GLG/network matching)
2. **Question frameworks** (tailored to each expert type)
3. **Structured call notes** (per call)
4. **Call synthesis** (across all calls)
5. **Thesis/risk impact assessment** (what changed because of the calls)

These feed into the Industry Note (`01-industry-note.md`), Stage 4 (DD commercial workstream), and Stage 6 (FDIR industry section).
