# Stage 3.1: Industry Note

You are writing a credit-focused industry note. This is NOT an equity research report — you are not arguing for upside potential. You are answering: **"Does this industry support reliable, predictable cash flow over 3-5 years?"**

---

## Industry Note Structure

**Target length: 5-10 pages.** Concise, data-heavy, focused on credit-relevant factors.

### Section 1: Industry Overview (1 page)

| Element | What to Cover |
|---------|--------------|
| **Market Definition** | What exactly is this market? Define boundaries clearly |
| **Market Size** | Current size in INR Cr (cite source). Don't use TAM/SAM/SOM (equity language) |
| **Growth Rate** | Historical 5-year CAGR + projected 3-5 year growth. Cite source |
| **Key Drivers** | 3-4 demand drivers. Which are structural (durable) vs. cyclical (temporary)? |
| **Value Chain** | Where in the value chain does the borrower sit? Who are upstream/downstream players? |

### Section 2: Competitive Landscape (1-2 pages)

**Peer Comparison Table:**

```
| Company | Revenue (INR Cr) | EBITDA Margin | Market Share | Leverage | Rating |
|---------|-----------------|---------------|-------------|----------|--------|
| Borrower | ___ | ___% | ___% | ___x | ___ |
| Peer 1 | ___ | ___% | ___% | ___x | ___ |
| Peer 2 | ___ | ___% | ___% | ___x | ___ |
| Peer 3 | ___ | ___% | ___% | ___x | ___ |
| Industry Avg | ___ | ___% | — | ___x | — |
```

**Competitive Dynamics:**
- Barriers to entry (high/medium/low — with evidence)
- Pricing power (can players pass through input cost increases?)
- Switching costs for customers
- Capacity utilization across industry (are players running full? Is overcapacity coming?)
- New entrant threats (who's building capacity? When does it come online?)

**Example (Asandas — Frozen Potato):**
```
Total India FF capacity: ~500k MT by CY25
Domestic consumption: ~125k MT (rest is export)
Industry utilization: ~50% average, but top 3 at >90%
New entrants struggling: Falcon invested in 20 MTPH plant but lacks potato supply
Key moat: Backward integration (seed pipeline takes 5 years to build)
```

### Section 3: Regulatory Environment (0.5-1 page)

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| **Key Regulators** | Who regulates this industry? (RBI, SEBI, FSSAI, MoEF, etc.) |
| **Recent Regulatory Changes** | Any policy shifts in last 2 years? |
| **Upcoming Regulatory Risk** | What's on the horizon? (Draft guidelines, parliamentary bills) |
| **Compliance Burden** | How heavy is the regulatory load? Does it favor incumbents? |

**For NBFC deals, this section is critical:**
- RBI master direction for NBFCs (scale-based regulation)
- Digital lending guidelines (Sep 2022 framework)
- Co-lending guidelines
- Provisioning norms (NPA classification, write-off policy)
- CRAR requirements by NBFC category

### Section 4: Industry Risk Assessment (1-2 pages)

Map industry-specific risks to credit impact:

| Risk | Probability | Credit Impact | Mitigant |
|------|------------|---------------|----------|
| Commodity price spike | Medium | Margin compression → DSCR pressure | Pass-through ability, hedging |
| Regulatory tightening | Low-Medium | Compliance cost increase | Incumbent advantage, scale |
| New capacity entry | Medium | Price competition → margin squeeze | Moat (brand, backward integration) |
| Export market disruption | Medium | Revenue concentration hit | Diversification into domestic |
| Technology disruption | Low | Obsolescence risk | Sector maturity, long asset life |
| Cyclical downturn | Medium | Volume and margin decline | Essential product, counter-cyclical demand |

### Section 5: Sector-Specific Credit Benchmarks (1 page)

What do "good" credit metrics look like in this industry?

**For Manufacturing:**
```
| Metric | Strong | Adequate | Weak |
|--------|--------|----------|------|
| EBITDA Margin | >20% | 12-20% | <12% |
| DSCR | >2.0x | 1.3-2.0x | <1.3x |
| Net Debt/EBITDA | <2.5x | 2.5-4.5x | >4.5x |
| WC Days | <90 | 90-150 | >150 |
| Capex/Revenue | <8% | 8-15% | >15% |
```

**For NBFC / Fintech:**
```
| Metric | Strong | Adequate | Weak |
|--------|--------|----------|------|
| NIM | >8% | 5-8% | <5% |
| Credit Cost | <3% | 3-6% | >6% |
| GNPA | <3% | 3-6% | >6% |
| CRAR | >20% | 15-20% | <15% |
| Collection Efficiency | >95% | 90-95% | <90% |
```

**For Food/Agri:**
```
| Metric | Strong | Adequate | Weak |
|--------|--------|----------|------|
| EBITDA Margin | >15% | 10-15% | <10% |
| Revenue Visibility | >6 months order book | 3-6 months | <3 months |
| WC Days | <120 | 120-180 | >180 |
| Capacity Utilization | >80% | 60-80% | <60% |
| Customer Concentration | Top 5 <40% | 40-60% | >60% (Asandas: >90% — Red Flag) |
```

### Section 6: Credit Conclusion (0.5 page)

Binary assessment: **Does this industry support the borrower's debt repayment?**

```
Industry Assessment: SUPPORTIVE / NEUTRAL / ADVERSE

Rationale:
- [2-3 sentences summarizing the credit-relevant conclusion]
- [Specific reference to how industry dynamics affect the borrower's ability to service debt]

Key Monitoring Items:
- [What industry factors should be tracked during facility life?]
```

## Research Sources

| Source Type | Where to Find | What It Tells You |
|------------|--------------|-------------------|
| **Industry Reports** | IBEF, CRISIL, ICRA, Fitch, S&P | Market size, growth, competitive landscape |
| **Trade Associations** | Sector-specific bodies | Policy positions, industry data, member lists |
| **Peer Company Filings** | MCA (annual reports), BSE/NSE (listed peers) | Financial benchmarks, competitive dynamics |
| **Credit Ratings** | CRISIL, ICRA, CARE, India Ratings | Peer credit quality, sector outlook |
| **RBI Data** | RBI website, NBFC data repository | NBFC sector data, systemic risk indicators |
| **Trade Publications** | Industry journals, trade media | Capacity additions, pricing trends, policy changes |
| **Expert Calls** | GLG, AlphaSights, Third Bridge | Practitioner perspective, competitive intelligence |

---

## Output

The industry note produces:
1. **5-10 page industry note** (all sections above)
2. **Peer comparison table** with financial benchmarks
3. **Industry risk register** (credit-relevant risks with probability and impact)
4. **Credit benchmarks** (what "good" looks like in this sector)
5. **Credit conclusion** (Supportive / Neutral / Adverse)

This feeds into Stage 4 (DD commercial workstream), Stage 5 (Model assumptions), and Stage 6 (FDIR industry section).
